Exercise 3.

Which of the following are one-to-one, onto, or both?
  1. \(f_1:\mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) defined by \(f_1(x) = x^3 - x\text{.}\)
  2. \(f_2 :\mathbb{Z} \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}\) defined by \(f_2(x)= -x + 2\text{.}\)
  3. \(f_3:\mathbb{N} \times \mathbb{N}\to \mathbb{N}\) defined by \(f_3(j, k) =2^j3^k\text{.}\)
  4. \(f_4 :\mathbb{P} \rightarrow \mathbb{P}\) defined by \(f_4(n)=\lceil n/2\rceil\text{,}\) where \(\lceil x\rceil\) is the ceiling of \(x\text{,}\) the smallest integer greater than or equal to \(x\text{.}\)
  5. \(f_5 :\mathbb{N} \rightarrow \mathbb{N}\) defined by \(f_5(n)=n^2+n\text{.}\)
  6. \(f_6:\mathbb{N} \rightarrow \mathbb{N} \times \mathbb{N}\) defined by \(f_6(n)= (2n, 2n+1)\text{.}\)
Answer.
  1. \(f_1\) is onto but not one-to-one: \(f_1(0)=f_1(1)\text{.}\)
  2. \(f_2\) is one-to-one and onto.
  3. \(f_3\) is one-to-one but not onto.
  4. \(f_4\) is onto but not one-to-one.
  5. \(f_5\) is one-to-one but not onto.
  6. \(f_6\) is one-to-one but not onto.
