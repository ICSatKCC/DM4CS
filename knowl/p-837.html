Define the events
\begin{equation*} A = \{ i \in S : i \text{ is divisible by } 2 \} \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} B = \{ i \in S : i \text{ is divisible by } 3 \} . \end{equation*}
Then we want to determine \(P(A \cup B)\text{,}\) which, by TheoremÂ 3.1.14 is equal to
\begin{equation*} P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B) . \end{equation*}
Since there are \(\lfloor 1000/2 \rfloor = 500\) even numbers in \(S\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} P(A) = 500/1000 . \end{equation*}
Since there are \(\lfloor 1000/3 \rfloor = 333\) elements in \(S\) that are divisible by \(3\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} P(B) = 333/1000 . \end{equation*}
Observe that \(i\) belongs to \(A \cap B\) if and only if \(i\) is divisible by \(6\text{,}\) i.e.,
\begin{equation*} A \cap B = \{ i \in S : i \text{ is divisible by } 6 \} . \end{equation*}
Since there are \(\lfloor 1000/6 \rfloor = 166\) elements in \(S\) that are divisible by \(6\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} P(A \cap B) = 166/1000 . \end{equation*}
We conclude that
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rl} P(A \cup B) &= P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B) \\ &= 500/1000 + 333/1000 - 166/1000 \\ &= 667/1000 . \end{array} \end{equation*}
