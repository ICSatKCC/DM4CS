Answer 3.1.3.7.1.

\(|S| = 2^{2n} = (2^2)^n = 4^n\) because each of the \(2n\) flips has 2 possible outcomes.
  1. Let \(A\) be the set of all sequences in \(S\) that have exactly \(n\) heads.
  2. We must determine \(|A|\text{.}\) To do this we must choose \(n\) flips to come up heads, out of \(2n\) flips total.
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rl} |A| = & {2n \choose n} \\ \end{array} \end{equation*}
    Therefore \(P(A)=\frac{|A|}{|S|} = \frac{2n \choose n}{4^n}\)
