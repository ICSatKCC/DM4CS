Let \(X\) be a random variable with expected value \(\mathbb{E}(X)\text{.}\) Then the variance of \(X\text{,}\) denoted by \(V(X)\) or \(\sigma^2\text{,}\) is
\begin{equation*} V(X) = \sum_{s \in S}(X(s) - \mathbb{E}(X))^2 \cdot P(s). \end{equation*}
Note that because of the squaring, the variance is not in the same units as \(X(s)\) and \(\mathbb{E}(X)\text{.}\) A low variance indicates that the values of \(X\) tend to be close to the expected value, while a large variance indicates that \(X\)â€™s outcomes are spread out over a wider range.
