Exercise 5.

Determine whether each of the functions: \(\log(3n + 2)\) and \(\log(n^3 + 1)\) is \(O(\log n)\) by finding a valid \(c\) and \(k\) for each, or showing they do not exist.
Answer.
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c c} \log(3n + 2) & \leq \log(3n + n), \forall n > 2\\ & = \log(4n)\\ & = \log 4 + \log n\\ & \leq \log n + \log n, \text{ if } n > 4.\\ & = 2 \log n. \end{array} \end{equation*}
Therefore \(\log(3n + 2)\) is \(O(\log n)\) with \(c = 2, k = 4\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c c} \log(n^3 + 1) & \leq \log(n^3 + n^3), \forall n > 1\\ & = \log(2n^3)\\ & = \log 2 + \log n^3\\ & = \log 2 + 3\log n\\ & \leq 4 \log n, \text{ if } n > 2. \end{array} \end{equation*}
Therefore \(\log(n^3 + 1)\) is \(O(\log n)\) with \(c = 4, k = 2\text{.}\)
