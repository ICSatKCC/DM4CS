Definition 3.1.12. Complement of an event.

If \(A\) is an event, then \(\overline{A}\) denotes its complement, i.e., \(\overline{A} = S - A\text{.}\) Intuitively, the sum of \(P(A)\) and \(P \left( \overline{A} \right)\) must be equal to one, because the event \(A\) either occurs or does not occur.
