Let \(A=\left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & -1 \\ 2 & 3 \\ \end{array} \right)\text{,}\) \(B =\left( \begin{array}{cc} 0 & 1 \\ 3 & -5 \\ \end{array} \right)\) , and \(C=\left( \begin{array}{ccc} 0 & 1 & -1 \\ 3 & -2 & 2 \\ \end{array} \right)\)
  1. Compute \(A B\) and \(B A\text{.}\)
  2. Compute \(A + B\) and \(B + A\text{.}\)
  3. If \(c = 3\text{,}\) show that \(c(A + B) = c A + c B\text{.}\)
  4. Show that \((A B)C = A(B C)\text{.}\)
  5. Compute \(A^2 C\text{.}\)
  6. Compute \(B + \pmb{0}\text{.}\)
  7. Compute \(A \pmb{0}_{2\times 2}\) and \(\pmb{0}_{2\times 2} A\text{,}\) where \(\pmb{0}_{2\times 2}\) is the \(2\times 2\) zero matrix.
  8. Compute \(0A\text{,}\) where 0 is the real number (scalar) zero.
  9. Let \(c = 2\) and \(d = 3\text{.}\) Show that \((c + d)A = c A + d A\text{.}\)
