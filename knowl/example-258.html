Example 17.2.4.

Let \(\Sigma = \ab\text{,}\) and consider the regular expression \(r=(a\REOR aa\REOR aaa)(bb)^*\text{.}\) Since \(L(a) = \{a\}\text{,}\) \(L(aa) = L(a)L(a) = \{aa\}\text{.}\) Similarly, \(L(aaa) = \{aaa\}\) and \(L(bb) = \{bb\}\text{.}\) Now \(L(a\REOR aa\REOR aaa) = L(a) \cup L(aa) \cup L(aaa) = \{a, aa, aaa\},\) and \(L((bb)^*) = (L((bb)))^* = (L(bb))^*\) (the last equality is from clause 7 of Definition 17.2.2), and \((L(bb))^* = \{bb\}^* = \{\varep, bb, bbbb, \ldots\}\text{.}\) So \(L(r)\) is the set of strings formed by concatenating \(a\) or \(aa\) or \(aaa\) with zero or more pairs of \(b\)’s.
