\((\Rightarrow )\) Assume that \((x,y) \in A\times C\text{.}\) Then \(x \in A\) and \(y \in C\) by definition of Cartesian product. Since \(A \subseteq B\text{,}\) \(x\in B\) by the definition of \(\subseteq\text{.}\) Then \((x,y) \in B\times C\) by definition of Cartesian product. Therefore, \(A\times C \subseteq B\times C\) by the definition of \(\subseteq\text{.}\) \(\square\)
