Item c.
Recursion: Let \(T_1, T_2,\ldots,T_r\text{,}\) \(r\geq 1\text{,}\) be disjoint rooted trees with roots \(v_1\text{,}\) \(v_2, \ldots \text{,}\) \(v_r\text{,}\) respectively, and let \(v_0\) be a vertex that does not belong to any of these trees. Then a rooted tree, rooted at \(v_0\text{,}\) is obtained by making \(v_0\) the parent of the vertices \(v_1\) , \(v_2, \ldots\text{,}\) and \(v_r\text{.}\) We call \(T_1, T_2, \ldots, T_r\) subtrees of the larger tree.
