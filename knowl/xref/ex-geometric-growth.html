Example 8.4.6. Geometric Growth Sequence.

Define the sequence of numbers \(B\) by
\begin{equation*} B_0 = 100\textrm{ and } \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} B_k = 1.08 B_{k-1} \textrm{ for } k\geq 1 \text{.} \end{equation*}
These rules stipulate that each number in the list is 1.08 times the previous number, with the starting number equal to 100. For example
\begin{equation*} \begin{split} B_3 &= 1.08 B_2 \\ &=1.08\left(1.08B_1\right)\\ &= 1.08\left(1.08\left(1.08 B_0\right)\right)\\ & = 1.08(1.08(1.08\cdot 100))\\ &= 1.08^3 100 = 125.971 \end{split} \end{equation*}
