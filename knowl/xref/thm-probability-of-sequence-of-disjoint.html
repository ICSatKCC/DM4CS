Theorem 3.1.10. Probability of a Sequence of Disjoint Events.

If \(A_1,A_2,\ldots,A_n\) is a sequence of pairwise disjoint events, then
\begin{equation*} P ( A_1 \cup A_2 \cup \cdots \cup A_n ) = \sum_{i=1}^n P\left(A_i\right) . \end{equation*}

Proof.

Define \(A = A_1 \cup A_2 \cup \cdots \cup A_n\text{.}\) Using DefinitionÂ 3.1.4, we have
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rl} P(A) &= \sum_{x \in A} P(x) \\ &= \sum_{i=1}^n \sum_{x \in A_i} P(x) \\ &= \sum_{i=1}^n P\left(A_i\right) . \end{array} \end{equation*}
