Theorem 3.1.13. Probability of Complements.

For any event \(A\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} P(A) = 1 - P \left( \bar{A} \right) . \end{equation*}

Proof.

Since \(A\) and \(\bar{A}\) are disjoint and \(S = A \cup \bar{A}\text{,}\) it follows from Theorem 3.1.10 that
\begin{equation*} P(S) = P \left( A \cup \bar{A} \right) = P(A) + P \left( \bar{A} \right) . \end{equation*}
We have seen in Definition 3.1.4 that \(P(S) = 1\text{.}\)
