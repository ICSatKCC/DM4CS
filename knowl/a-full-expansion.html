Example 2.4.11. A Binomial Expansion.

Expand \((3 x - 2 )^{3}\text{.}\) If we replace \(x\) and \(y\) in the Binomial Theorem with \(3x\) and \(-2\text{,}\) respectively, we get
\begin{equation*} \begin{split} \sum_{k=0}^3 \binom{3}{k} (3x)^{n-k} (-2)^k & = \binom{3}{0} (3x)^{3} (-2)^0 + \binom{3}{1} (3x)^{2} (-2)^1 + \binom{3}{2} (3x)^{1} (-2)^2 + \binom{3}{3} (3x)^{0} (-2)^3 \\ & = 27 x^3 - 54 x^2 + 36 x - 8 \end{split}\text{.} \end{equation*}
