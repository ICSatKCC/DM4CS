Let \((S,P)\) be a probability space, let \(n\geq 2\text{,}\) and let \(X_1,X_2,\ldots,X_n\) be a sequence of random variables on \(S\text{.}\)
  1. We say that this sequence is pairwise independent if for all real numbers \(x_1,x_2,\ldots,x_n\text{,}\) the sequence “\(X_1=x_1\)”, “\(X_2=x_2\)”, \(\ldots\text{,}\) “\(X_n=x_n\)” of events is pairwise independent.
  2. We say that this sequence is mutually independent if for all real numbers \(x_1,x_2,\ldots,x_n\text{,}\) the sequence “\(X_1=x_1\)”, “\(X_2=x_2\)”, \(\ldots\text{,}\) “\(X_n=x_n\)” of events is mutually independent.
