If we let \(p\) represent “The money is behind Door A” and \(q\) represent “The money is behind Door B,” \(p \Rightarrow (p \lor q)\) is a formalized version of the reasoning used in Example 4.3.12. A common name for this implication is disjunctive addition. In the next section we will consider some of the most commonly used implications and equivalences.
