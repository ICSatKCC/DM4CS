If \(n\) and \(k\) are nonnegative integers with \(0 \leq k \leq n\text{,}\) then the number \(k\)-combinations from a set of \(n\) elements with repetition allowed is equal to
\begin{equation*} \binom{n+k-1}{k} = \binom{n+k-1}{n-1} =\frac{(n+k-1)!}{(n-1)! \cdot k!} \text{.} \end{equation*}
