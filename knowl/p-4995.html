It may not be easy, looking at a formal specification of a DFA, to determine what language that automaton accepts. Fortunately, the mathematical description of the automaton \(M=(Q, \Sigma, q_0, \delta, F)\) can be neatly and helpfully captured in a picture called a transition diagram. Consider again the DFA of the two preceding examples. Looking at this picture, it should be fairly easy to see that the language accepted by the DFA \(M\) is \(L(M) = \{ x \in \{0,1\}^* \ | \ n_1(x) \geq 2\}\text{.}\)
