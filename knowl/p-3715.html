Consider the graph, \(G\text{,}\) in the top row of Figure 13.1.18. The other three graphs in that figure are all subgraphs of \(G\text{.}\) The graph in the bottom left was created by first removing vertex 5 and all edges connecting it. In addition, we have removed the edge \(\{1,4\}\text{.}\) That removed edge disqualifies the graph from being an induced subgraph. The graphs in the bottom center and right are both spanning subgraphs. The one on the bottom right is a tree, and is referred to as a spanning subtree. Spanning subtrees will be discussed in Section 14.2.
