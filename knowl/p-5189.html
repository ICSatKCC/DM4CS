A vertical bar, \(\BNFALT\text{,}\) indicates a choice of alternatives. For example,
\begin{equation*} \langle digit \rangle ::=\ 0 \BNFALT\ 1 \BNFALT\ 2 \BNFALT\ 3 \BNFALT\ 4 \BNFALT\ 5 \BNFALT\ 6 \BNFALT\ 7 \BNFALT\ 8 \BNFALT\ 9 \end{equation*}
indicates that the non-terminal \(\langle digit \rangle\) can be replaced by any one of the terminal symbols \(0, 1, \dots,9\text{.}\)
