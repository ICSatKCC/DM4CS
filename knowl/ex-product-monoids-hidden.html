Consider the monoids \(\mathbb{N}\) (the set of natural numbers with addition) and \(B^*\) (the set of finite strings of 0’s and 1’s with concatenation). The direct product of \(\mathbb{N}\) with \(B^*\) is a monoid. We illustrate its operation, which we will denote by \(\bullet \text{,}\) with examples:
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c} (4, 001)\bullet(3, 11) = (4 + 3, 001+11) = (7, 00111)\\ (0, 11010)\bullet(3,01) = (3, 1101001)\\ (0, \lambda )\bullet(129, 00011) = (0 + 129, \lambda +00011) = (129, 00011)\\ (2, 01)\bullet(8, 10) = (10,0110)\textrm{, and }\\ (8, 10)\bullet(2, 01) = (10, 1001) \end{array} \end{equation*}
Note that our new monoid is not commutative. What is the identity for \(\bullet \) ?
