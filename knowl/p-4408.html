Although the closest neighbor circuit is often not optimal, we may be satisfied if it is close to optimal. If \(C_{opt}\) and \(C_{cn}\) are the costs of optimal and closest neighbor circuits in a graph, then it is always the case that \(C_{opt}\leq C_{cn}\) or \(\frac{C_{cn}}{C_{opt}}\geq 1\text{.}\) We can assess how good the closest neighbor algorithm is by determining how small the quantity \(\frac{C_{cn}}{C_{opt}}\) gets. If it is always near 1, then the algorithm is good. However, if there are graphs for which it is large, then the algorithm may be discarded.
