In general a linear system is of the form
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c c c c c c} a_{11}x_1 + &\dots & + a_{1n}x_n & = b_1 \\ &\vdots & & \vdots \\ a_{m1}x_1 + &\dots & + a_{mn}X_n & = b_m \\ \end{array} \end{equation*}
where the \(x_i\) are variables and the \(a_{ij}\) and \(b_i\) are constants. This system can be represented by the augmented matrix
\begin{equation*} \left( \begin{array}{c c c | c} a_{11} & \dots & a_{1n} & b_1 \\ & \vdots & & \vdots \\ a_{m1} & \dots & a_{mn} & b_m \\ \end{array} \right). \end{equation*}
Changes to a system of equations as a result of an elementary operation translate into changes of an augmented matrix resulting from a row operation.
