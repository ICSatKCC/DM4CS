Item.
Solving a system of simultaneous equations such as \(x + y = 7\) and \(x - y = 3\) can be viewed as an intersection. Let
\begin{equation*} A = \{(x,y): x + y = 7, x,y \in \mathbb{R}\} \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} B = \{(x,y): x - y = 3, x,y\in \mathbb{R}\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
These two sets are lines in the plane and their intersection, \(A \cap B = \{(5, 2)\}\text{,}\) is the solution to the system.
