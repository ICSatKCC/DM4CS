Definition 1.2.1. Intersection.

Let \(A\) and \(B\) be sets. The intersection of \(A\) and \(B\) (denoted by \(A \cap B\)) is the set of all elements that are in both \(A\) and \(B\text{.}\) That is,
\begin{equation*} A \cap B = \{x:x \in A \textrm{ and } x \in B\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
