What is the conditional probability \(P(A \mid B)\text{?}\) To determine this probability, we assume that event \(B\) occurs, i.e., the roll of the die resulted in one of \(1\text{,}\) \(3\text{,}\) and \(5\text{.}\) Given that event \(B\) occurs, event \(A\) occurs in one out of these three possibilities. Thus, \(P(A \mid B)\) should be equal to \(1/3\text{.}\) We are going to verify that this is indeed the answer we get when using DefinitionÂ 3.2.1: Since
\begin{equation*} A = \{ 3 \} \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} B = \{ 1,3,5 \} , \end{equation*}
we have
\begin{equation*} P(A \mid B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} = \frac{P(A)}{P(B)} = \frac{|A|/|S|}{|B|/|S|} = \frac{1/6}{3/6} = 1/3 . \end{equation*}
