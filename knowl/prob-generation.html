Problem 15.2.11. The Generation Problem.

Design an algorithm that generates or produces any string in \(L\text{.}\) Here we presume that \(A\) is either finite or countably infinite; hence, \(A^*\) is countable by TheoremÂ 15.2.3, and \(L \subseteq A^*\) must be countable. Therefore, the generation of \(L\) amounts to creating a list of strings in \(L\text{.}\) The list may be either finite or infinite, and you must be able to show that every string in \(L\) appears somewhere in the list.
