Exercise 8.

Show that each of the following grammars is ambiguous by finding a string that has two left derivations according to the grammar:
  1. \begin{align*} S \amp \PRODUCES SS \\ S \amp \PRODUCES aSb\\ S \amp \PRODUCES bSa \\ S \amp\PRODUCES\EMPTYSTRING \end{align*}
  2. \begin{align*} S \amp \PRODUCES ASb \\ S \amp \PRODUCES \EMPTYSTRING\\ A \amp \PRODUCES aA\\ A \amp\PRODUCES a \end{align*}
Answer.
There are many answers to these.
  1. \(abab \)
    \begin{align*} S \amp \YIELDS SS \\ \amp \YIELDS aSbS \\ \amp \YIELDS aSb \\ \amp \YIELDS abaSb \\ \amp \YIELDS abab \end{align*}
    \begin{align*} S \amp \YIELDS aSb \\ \amp \YIELDS abSab \\ \amp \YIELDS abab \end{align*}
  2. \(aab\)
    \begin{align*} S \amp \YIELDS ASb \\ \amp \YIELDS aASb \\ \amp \YIELDS aaSb \\ \amp \YIELDS aab \end{align*}
    \begin{align*} S \amp \YIELDS ASb \\ \amp \YIELDS Ab \\ \amp \YIELDS aAb \\ \amp \YIELDS aab \end{align*}
