\((\Rightarrow )\)(Indirect) Assume that \(\overline{B}\) is not a subset of \(\overline{A}\) . Therefore, there exists \(x\in \overline{B}\) that does not belong to \(\overline{A}\text{.}\) \(x \notin \overline{A} \Rightarrow x \in A\text{.}\) Therefore, \(x\in A\) and \(x\notin B\text{,}\) a contradiction to the assumption that \(A\subseteq B\text{.}\) \(\square\)
