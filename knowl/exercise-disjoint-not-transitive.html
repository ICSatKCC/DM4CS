Exercise 6.

Let \(A\text{,}\) \(B\) and \(C\) be sets. Prove or disprove:
\begin{equation*} A \cap B \neq \emptyset, B \cap C \neq \emptyset \Rightarrow A\cap C \neq \emptyset \end{equation*}
Answer.
The statement is false. The sets \(A=\{1,2\}\text{,}\) \(B=\{2,3\}\)\ and \(C=\{3,4\}\)\ provide a counterexample. Looking ahead to ICS 241, in the chapter on relations, we would say that the relation of being non-disjoint is not transitive.
