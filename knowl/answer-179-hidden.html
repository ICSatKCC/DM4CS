Basis: (\(n = 2\)) \(\left(a_1\bullet a_2\right)^{-1}= a_2^{-1}\bullet a_1^{-1}\) by TheoremÂ 11.3.7.
Induction: Assume that for some \(n \geq 2\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} \left(a_1\bullet a_2\bullet \cdots \bullet a_n\right)^{-1}=a_n^{-1}\bullet \cdots \bullet a_2^{-1}\bullet a_1^{-1} \end{equation*}
We must show that
\begin{equation*} \left(a_1\bullet a_2\bullet \cdots \bullet a_n\bullet a_{n+1}\right)^{-1}=a_{n+1}^{-1}\bullet a_n^{-1}\bullet \cdots \bullet a_2^{-1}\bullet a_1^{-1} \end{equation*}
This can be accomplished as follows:
\begin{equation*} \begin{split} \left(a_1\bullet a_2\bullet \cdots \bullet a_n\bullet a_{n+1}\right)^{-1} &=\left(\left(a_1\bullet a_2\bullet \cdots \bullet a_n\right)\bullet a_{n+1}\right)^{-1}\textrm{ by the associative law}\\ &=a_{n+1}^{-1}\bullet \left(a_1\bullet a_2\bullet \cdots \bullet a_n\right)^{-1}\textrm{ by the basis}\\ &=a_{n+1}^{-1}\bullet \left(a_n^{-1}\bullet \cdots \bullet a_2^{-1}\bullet a_1^{-1}\right) \textrm{ by the induction hypothesis}\\ &= a_{n+1}^{-1}\bullet a_n^{-1}\bullet \cdots \bullet a_2^{-1}\bullet a_1^{-1} \textrm{ by the associative law}\\ \end{split} \end{equation*}
