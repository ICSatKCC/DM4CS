Theorem 3.3.7. Comparison of Expectations.
Let \((S,P)\) be a probability space and let \(X\) and \(Y\) be two random variables on \(S\text{.}\) If \(X \leq Y\text{,}\) then \(\mathbb{E}(X) \leq \mathbb{E}(Y)\text{.}\)
Proof.
Using DefinitionÂ 3.3.1 and the assumption that \(X \leq Y\text{,}\) we obtain
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rl} \mathbb{E}(X) & = \sum_{s \in S} X(s) \cdot P(s) \\ & \leq & \sum_{s \in S} Y(s) \cdot P(s) \\ & = \mathbb{E}(Y) . \end{array} \end{equation*}
