Theorem 11.5.3. Subgroup Conditions.

To determine whether H, a subset of group \([G;\bullet ]\text{,}\) is a subgroup, it is sufficient to prove:
  1. H is closed under \(\bullet \text{;}\) that is, \(a, b \in H \Rightarrow a \bullet b \in H\text{;}\)
  2. H contains the identity element for \(\bullet \text{;}\) and
  3. H contains the inverse of each of its elements; that is, \(a\in H \Rightarrow a^{-1}\in H\text{.}\)
