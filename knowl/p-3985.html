In words, (13.3.13) says that the generating function of the sum of two sequences equals the sum of the generating functions of those sequences. Take the time to write out the other four identities in your own words. From the previous examples, these identities should be fairly obvious, with the possible exception of the last two. We will prove (13.3.16) as part of the next theorem and leave the proof of (13.3.17) to the interested reader. Note that there is no operation on generating functions that is related to sequence multiplication; that is, \(G(S\cdot T;z)\) cannot be simplified.
