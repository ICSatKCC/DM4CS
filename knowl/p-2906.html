Sometimes it is helpful to illustrate a relation with a graph. Consider Example 10.1.2. A graph of \(R\) can be drawn as in Figure 10.1.6. The arrows indicate that 1 is related to 4 under \(R\text{.}\) Also, 2 is related to 4 under \(R\text{,}\) and 3 is related to 5, while the upper arrow denotes that \(R\) is a relation from the whole set \(A\) into the set \(B\text{.}\)
