Answer 11.1.4.3.1.

\begin{equation*} \begin{split} a, b \in A \cap B & \Rightarrow a, b \in A \textrm{ by the definition of intersection}\\ & \Rightarrow a\bullet b \in A\textrm{ by the closure of } A \textrm{ with respect to } \bullet \end{split} \end{equation*}
Similarly, \(a, b \in A \cap B \Rightarrow a\bullet b \in B\text{.}\) Therefore, \(a \bullet b \in A \cap B\text{.}\) The set of positive integers is closed under addition, and so is the set of negative integers, but \(1 + -1 - 0\text{.}\) Therefore, their union, the nonzero integers, is not closed under addition.
