Exercise 5.

Use mathematical induction to show that for \(n\geq 1\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{1\cdot 2 }+ \frac{1}{2\cdot 3}+ \cdots + \frac{1}{n(n+1)}= \frac{n}{n+1}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Answer.
Basis: For \(n=1\text{,}\) we observe that \(\frac{1}{(1\cdot 2)}=\frac{1}{(1+1)}\)
Induction: Assume that for some \(n\geqslant 1\text{,}\) the formula is true.
Then:
\begin{equation*} \begin{split} \frac{1}{(1\cdot 2)}+\cdots +\frac{1}{((n+1)(n+2))} &=\frac{n}{(n+1)}+\frac{1}{((n+1)(n+2))}\\ &=\frac{(n+2)(n)}{(n+1)(n+2)}+\frac{1}{(n+1)(n+2)}\\ &=\frac{(n+1)^2}{((n+1)(n+2))}\\ &=\frac{(n+1)}{(n+2)} \quad \blacksquare\\ \end{split} \end{equation*}
