Let \(a \in L\) and assume to the contrary that \(a\) has two complements, namely \(a_1\) and \(a_2\text{.}\) Then by the definition of complement,
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c} a \land a_1 = 0\textrm{ and }a \lor a_1 = 1, \\ and\\ a \land a_2 = 0\textrm{ and }a \lor a_2 = 1, \\ \end{array} \end{equation*}
