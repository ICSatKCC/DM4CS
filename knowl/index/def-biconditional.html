Definition 4.1.10. Biconditional Proposition.

If \(p\) and \(q\) are propositions, the biconditional statement “\(p\) if and only if \(q\text{,}\)” denoted \(p \leftrightarrow q\text{,}\) is defined by the truth table
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|c|c|c|} \hline p & q & p\leftrightarrow q \\ \hline 0 & 0 & 1 \\ \hline 0 & 1 & 0 \\ \hline 1 & 0 & 0 \\ \hline 1 & 1 & 1 \\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}
