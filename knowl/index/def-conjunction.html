Definition 4.1.3. Logical Conjunction (AND).

If \(p\) and \(q\) are propositions, their conjunction, \(p \textrm{ and } q\) (denoted \(p \land q\)), is defined by the truth table
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|c|c|c|} \hline p & q & p\land q \\ \hline 0 & 0 & 0 \\\hline 0 & 1 & 0 \\\hline 1 & 0 & 0 \\\hline 1 & 1 & 1 \\\hline \end{array} \end{equation*}
The statement \(p \land q\) is only true if both \(p\) and \(q\) are true.
