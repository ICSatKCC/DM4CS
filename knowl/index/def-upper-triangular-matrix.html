Definition 11.4.15.
A matrix \(M\) , is upper triangular if \(M_{ij}= 0\) whenever \(i \gt j\text{.}\) Thus such a matrix equals zero below the main diagonal, the entries of the form \(M_{ii}\text{,}\) as shown.
\begin{equation*} \begin{pmatrix} * & * & \cdots & *\\ 0 & * & \ddots & \vdots\\ \vdots & \ddots & \ddots & *\\ 0 & \cdots & 0 & * \end{pmatrix} \end{equation*}
A lower triangular matrix is defined similarly as a matrix for which all entries above the main diagonal are equal to zero.
