A coin is tossed twice. Consider the following events.
A
Heads on the first toss.
B
Heads on the second toss.
C
The two tosses come out the same.
  1. Show that \(A\text{,}\)~\(B\text{,}\)~\(C\) are pairwise independent but not independent.
  2. Show that \(C\) is independent of \(A\) and \(B\) but not of \(A \cap B\text{.}\)
