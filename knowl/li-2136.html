Item 3.
if \(r_1\) and \(r_2\) are regular expressions, then so are \(r_1\REOR r_2\) , \(r_1\cdot r_2\text{,}\) \(r_1^*\) and \((r_1)\) (and of course, \(r_2^*\) and \((r_2)\)). As in concatenation of strings, the \(\cdot\) is often left out of the second expression. (Note: the order of precedence of operators, from lowest to highest, is \(\REOR\) , \(\cdot\text{,}\) \(*\text{.}\))
