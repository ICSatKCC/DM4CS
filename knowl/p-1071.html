Let \(X\) be a random variable with variance \(V(X)\text{.}\) Then the standard deviation of \(X\) is
\begin{equation*} \sigma = \sqrt{V(X)} \end{equation*}
Like the variance, a low standard deviation indicates that the outcomes of an experiment, or values of \(X\) tend to be close to the expected value, while a high standard deviation indicates that the outcomes are spread out over a wider range of values. The standard deviation is often more useful than the variance because it is in the same units as \(X\) and \(\mathbb{E}(X)\) .
