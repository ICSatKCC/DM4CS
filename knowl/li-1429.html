Item 10.3.4.1.b.
\(\mid\) on \(A\) is reflexive because \(\forall a \in A, a \mid a\) is true and \((a,a) \in \) the relation. \(\mid\) on \(A\) is antisymmetric because \(\forall a, b \in A\) if \(a \mid b\) is true and \((a,b) \in \) the relation, then \(b \mid a\) is not true and \((b,a) \notin \) the relation, unless \(a = b\text{.}\) \(\mid\) on \(A\) is transitive because \(\forall a, b, c \in A\text{,}\) if \((a,b)\) and \((b,c) \in\) the relation, then \(a|b\) and \(b|c\text{.}\) Therefore \(ak =b\) for some integer \(k\) and \(bj = c\) for some integer \(j\text{.}\) Thus \(akj = c\text{,}\) \(a|c\text{,}\) and \((a,c) \in\) the relation. Therefore \(\mid\) on \(A\) is a partial ordering.
