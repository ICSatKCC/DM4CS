Let \(A\) and \(B\) be sets. The difference of set \(B\) and set \(A\) or the complement of \(A\) relative to \(B\) (notation \(B - A\)) is the set of elements that are in \(B\) and not in \(A\text{.}\) That is, \(B-A=\{x: x\in B \textrm{ and } x\notin A\}\text{.}\) If \(U\) is the universal set, then \(U-A\) is denoted by \(\overline{A}\) and is called simply the complement of \(A\text{.}\) \(\overline{A}=\{x\in U : x\notin A\}\text{.}\)
