If two numbers, \(m\) and \(n\text{,}\) differ by a multiple of \(n\text{,}\) we say that they are congruent modulo \(n\text{,}\) denoted \(a \equiv b\pmod{n}\text{.}\) For example, \(13 \equiv 38\pmod{5}\) because \(13-38 = -25\text{,}\) which is a multiple of 5.
