Theorem 15.1.4. Submonoid Test.

Assume \([M; \bullet ]\) is a monoid and \(K\) is a nonempty subset of \(M\text{.}\) Then \(K\) is a submonoid of \(M\) if and only if the following two conditions are met.
  • If \(a,b\in K\text{,}\) then. \(a\bullet b\in K\text{;}\) i. e., \(K\) is closed with under \(\bullet \text{.}\)
  • The identity of \(M\) belongs to \(K\text{.}\)
