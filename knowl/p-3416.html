Consider the poset \((\mathcal{P}(A),\subseteq)\) we examined in Example 12.1.10. It isn’t too surprising that every pair of sets had a greatest lower bound and least upper bound. Thus, we have a lattice in this case; and \(A\lor B = A \cup B\) and \(A\land B = A \cap B\text{.}\) The reader is encouraged to write out the operation tables \([\mathcal{P}(A); \cup, \cap]\text{.}\)
