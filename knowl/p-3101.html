Remark: A convenient help in constructing the adjacency matrix of a relation from a set \(A\) into a set \(B\) is to write the elements from \(A\) in a column preceding the first column of the adjacency matrix, and the elements of \(B\) in a row above the first row. Initially, \(M\) in ExampleÂ 2 would be
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{cc} & \begin{array}{ccc} 2 & 5 & 6 \\ \end{array} \\ \begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 5 \\ 6 \\ \end{array} & \left( \begin{array}{ccc} & & \\ & & \\ & & \\ \end{array} \right) \\ \end{array} \end{equation*}
To fill in the matrix, \(M_{ij}\) is 1 if and only if \(\left(a_i,b_j\right) \in R\text{.}\) So that, since the pair \((2, 5) \in R\text{,}\) the entry of \(M\) corresponding to the row labeled 2 and the column labeled 5 in the matrix is a 1.
