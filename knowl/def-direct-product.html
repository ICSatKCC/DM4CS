Definition 11.6.1. Direct Product.

If \(\left[V_i;\bullet_i, \diamond_i, \ldots \right]\text{,}\) \(i = 1, 2, \ldots, n\) are algebraic systems of the same kind, then the direct product of these systems is \(V =V_1\times V_2\times \cdots \times V_n\) , with operations defined below. The elements of \(V\) are \(n\)-tuples of the form \(\left(a_1, a_2, \dots , a _n \right)\text{,}\) where \(a_k \in V_k\text{,}\) \(k = 1, \dots , n\text{.}\) The systems \(V_1\text{,}\) \(V_2, \dots, V_n\) are called the factors of \(V\text{.}\) There are as many operations on \(V\) as there are in the factors. Each of these operations is defined componentwise:
If \(\left(a_1, a_2, . . . , a _n \right), \left(b_1,b_2, . . . , b _n \right)\in V\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c} \left(a_1, a_2, \dots , a_n \right) \bullet\left(b_1, b_2, \dots , b_n \right) = \left(a_1 \bullet_1 b_1, a_2 \bullet_2 b_2,\ldots , a_n \bullet_n b_n \right)\\ \left(a_1, a_2, \dots , a_n \right) \diamond \left(b_1, b_2, \dots , b_n \right) = \left(a_1 \diamond_1 b_1, a_2 \diamond_2 b_2,\dots , a_n \diamond_n b_n\right)\\ \textrm{etc.}\\ \\ \end{array} \end{equation*}
