As an example of a complete BNF grammar, let’s look at a BNF grammar for a very small subset of English. The start symbol for the grammar is \(\langle sentence \rangle\text{,}\) and the terminal symbols are English words. All the sentences that can be produced from this grammar are syntactically correct English sentences, although you wouldn’t encounter many of them in conversation. Here is the grammar:
\begin{align*} \langle sentence \rangle \amp ::=\ \langle simple{\text -}sentence \rangle [ and \langle simple{\text -}sentence \rangle ]\dots \\ \langle simple{\text -}sentence \rangle \amp ::=\ \langle noun{\text -}part \rangle \langle verb{\text -}part \rangle \\ \langle noun{\text -}part \rangle \amp ::=\ \langle article \rangle \langle noun \rangle [ who \langle verb{\text -}part \rangle ]\dots \\ \langle verb{\text -}part \rangle \amp ::=\ \langle intransitive{\text -}verb \rangle \BNFALT\ ( \langle transitive{\text -}verb \rangle \langle noun{\text -}part \rangle ) \\ \langle article \rangle \amp ::=\ the \BNFALT\ a \\ \langle noun \rangle \amp ::=\ man \BNFALT\ woman \BNFALT\ dog \BNFALT\ cat \BNFALT\ computer \\ \langle intransitive{\text -}verb \rangle \amp ::=\ runs \BNFALT\ jumps \BNFALT\ hides \\ \langle transitive{\text -}verb \rangle \amp ::=\ knows \BNFALT\ loves \BNFALT\ chases \BNFALT\ owns \end{align*}
This grammar can generate sentences such as “A dog chases the cat and the cat hides” and “The man loves a woman who runs.” The second sentence, for example, is generated by the derivation
\begin{align*} \langle sentence \rangle\ \amp \YIELDS\ \langle simple{\text -}sentence \rangle \\ \amp \YIELDS\ \langle noun{\text -}part \rangle\ \langle verb{\text -}part \rangle\\ \amp \YIELDS\ \langle article \rangle\ \langle noun \rangle\ \langle verb{\text -}part \rangle \\ \amp \YIELDS\ \mbox{the}\ \langle noun \rangle\ \langle verb{\text -}part \rangle\\ \amp \YIELDS\ \mbox{the}\ \mbox{man}\ \langle verb{\text -}part \rangle \\ \amp \YIELDS\ \mbox{the}\ \mbox{man}\ \langle transitive{\text -}verb \rangle\ \langle noun{\text -}part \rangle\\ \amp \YIELDS\ \mbox{the}\ \mbox{man}\ \mbox{loves}\ \langle noun{\text -}part \rangle \\ \amp \YIELDS\ \mbox{the}\ \mbox{man}\ \mbox{loves}\ \langle article \rangle \ \langle noun \rangle\ \mbox{who}\ \langle verb{\text -}part \rangle \\ \amp \YIELDS\ \mbox{the}\ \mbox{man}\ \mbox{loves}\ \mbox{a} \ \langle noun \rangle\ \mbox{who}\ \langle verb{\text -}part \rangle\\ \amp \YIELDS\ \mbox{the}\ \mbox{man}\ \mbox{loves}\ \mbox{a} \ \mbox{woman}\ \mbox{who}\ \langle verb{\text -}part \rangle\\ \amp \YIELDS\ \mbox{the}\ \mbox{man}\ \mbox{loves}\ \mbox{a} \ \mbox{woman}\ \mbox{who}\ \langle intransitive{\text -}verb \rangle\\ \amp \YIELDS\ \mbox{the}\ \mbox{man}\ \mbox{loves}\ \mbox{a} \ \mbox{woman}\ \mbox{who}\ \mbox{runs} \end{align*}
